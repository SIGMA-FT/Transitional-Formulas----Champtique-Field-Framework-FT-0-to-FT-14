## FT-1 — Field Decomposition: Condensed and Diffuse
**Status:** ACQUIRED
**Expression:** `Φ = Φc + Φd`

---

### Statement

The single field Φ decomposes into two structural states:

**Φc** — condensed field
Localized, stable, slow — the substrate of matter.

**Φd** — diffuse field
Extended, penetrating, fast — the carrier of memory and information.

---

### Critical Note on the Nature of This Decomposition

Condensed and diffuse are **not opposing entities**.
They are two states of a single continuum.

What classical physics calls "repulsion" is not a fundamental force —
it is the field's search for the continuum by a different geometric path.
Attraction remains the fundamental tendency at all scales.

This distinction is not terminological.
It is the structural consequence of the 1785 choice
documented in Deposit 2 (CMRPCh-Electromagnetic).

---

### Parameters

**Φc** — condensed component: ρ high, κ high, τm → ∞ locally
**Φd** — diffuse component: ρ low, κ variable, non-local

**Φd absorption hierarchy (by material type):**
```
water > living matter > air > vacuum
```
This hierarchy is documented in observables EXP-006, EXP-007, EXP-009.

---

### Observable Grounding

Every experiment in the corpus maps onto this decomposition:

- EXP-001 (electric fence): Φd organized in cylindrical structure around Φc conductor
- EXP-003 (split tube): Φd of magnet diffuses into Φc of conductor without circuit
- EXP-006 (water/cotton): Φd of water reorganizes at Φc interface → thermal trace
- EXP-013 (silver coin): Φd peripheral field measurable around Φc coin
- PH-039 (gravity): differential between any Φc body and ambient Φd background

---

### Falsification Criterion

FT-1 fails if:
any documented phenomenon requires a third irreducible field component
beyond Φc and Φd.
