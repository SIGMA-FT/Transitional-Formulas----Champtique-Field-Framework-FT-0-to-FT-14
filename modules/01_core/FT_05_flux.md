## FT-5 — Champtique Flux Without Material Transport
**Status:** ACQUIRED
**Expression:** `J = −∂tΦ`

---

### Statement

Flux is defined as temporal field variation.
It does not require material transport.

---

### Parameters

**J** — champtique flux
**∂tΦ** — temporal field variation at position x

---

### What This Resolves

Classical physics requires material transport or closed conducting circuits
to explain flux phenomena. FT-5 decouples flux from transport.

The split tube (EXP-003) is the direct demonstration:
field interaction without circumferential electrical continuity.
No circuit — flux nonetheless.

---

### Observable Grounding

- EXP-003 (split tube): braking without circuit — J exists without material current loop
- EXP-004 (rotating magnet drives copper tube): immediate coupling across air gap
- EXP-002 (Lewin capacitor): field maintained in relation between separated parts

---

### Falsification Criterion

FT-5 fails if:
flux is demonstrated to require material transport in all documented cases.
