## FT-14 — Champtique Differential as Universal Driver
**Status:** ACQUIRED
**Expression:** `ΔΦ = Φ₁ − Φ₂`

---

### Statement

The champtique differential between two zones or systems
is the fundamental driver of every observable phenomenon.
The larger the differential, the faster the onset and the stronger the effect.

---

### Parameters

**ΔΦ** — field differential between systems 1 and 2

**Regime scale:**
```
ΔΦ high → fast onset, strong effect
           (copper/ice fusion, explosion, thermal shock)
ΔΦ low  → slow onset, progressive effect
ΔΦ → 0  → return to continuum, phenomenon terminates
```

---

### Distinction from FT-3

FT-3 (O ∝ ∇Φ) captures **local** spatial variation within one system.
FT-14 (ΔΦ = Φ₁ − Φ₂) captures the differential **between two distinct systems**.

These are not the same. FT-14 is the inter-system motor.
FT-3 is the intra-system observable.

---

### Observable Grounding

FT-14 is the most widely applied formula in the corpus.
It unifies phenomena previously explained by unrelated mechanisms:

- **PH-001 (Mpemba)**: hot water ↔ cold environment = large ΔΦ → fast freezing onset
- **EXP-005 (copper/ice)**: extreme ΔΦ copper/ice → accelerated phase transition
- **PH-004 (water on burning oil)**: maximum ΔΦ → instantaneous brutal redistribution
- **PH-039 (gravity)**: ΔΦ between any condensed body and ambient Φd background (~200 V/m)
- **PH-012 (aluminothermy)**: extreme ΔΦ Al powder/iron oxide → brutal onset
- **PH-013 (dust explosions)**: fragmented condensed matter + air → maximum interface ΔΦ
- **EXP-006 to EXP-009**: thermal series — ΔΦ drives every interface phenomenon

---

### Falsification Criterion

FT-14 fails if:
a phase transition, interface phenomenon, or cross-system interaction
is demonstrated to be independent of the field differential ΔΦ
between the systems involved.
