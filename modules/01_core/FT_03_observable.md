## FT-3 — Observable as Field Gradient
**Status:** ACQUIRED
**Expression:** `O ∝ ∇Φ`

---

### Statement

Every measurable observable corresponds to a spatial variation of the field.

Force, apparent charge, tension, measured effect —
all are gradient structures, not independent fundamental entities.

---

### Parameters

**O** — any measurable observable (force, voltage, temperature signature, pressure...)
**∇Φ** — spatial field gradient at the measurement point

---

### What This Replaces

In the standard framework, multiple independent entities are introduced
(electric field, magnetic field, gravitational field, nuclear forces...)
each with its own formalism.

In Pch, all reduce to spatial variations of a single field Φ.
The different "forces" are different gradient geometries, different regimes —
not different substances.

---

### Observable Grounding

- EXP-001 (electric fence): cylindrical field structure around conductor
  = ∇Φ organized in a tube, not a tangential vector at a point
- PH-039 (gravity): ∇Φ between any condensed body and ambient Φd background (~200 V/m)
- PH-037 (GPS): clock rate difference = ∇Φ between orbital and surface field states
- EXP-006 (water/cotton): thermal front = ∇Φ propagation front

---

### Falsification Criterion

FT-3 fails if:
a documented observable is demonstrated to be irreducible
to a spatial field gradient.
