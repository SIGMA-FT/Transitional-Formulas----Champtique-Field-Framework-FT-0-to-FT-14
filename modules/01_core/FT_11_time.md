## FT-11 — Local Variable Time Cadence
**Status:** ACQUIRED
**Expression:** `dτ = Θ(ρ,κ,m) · dt`

---

### Statement

Time is not a fundamental dimension.
It emerges as a cadence of processes when the field
is sufficiently dense and coherent.

---

### Parameters

**dτ** — local proper time (measured by a local process)
**Θ(ρ,κ,m)** — geometric-champtique factor
**dt** — coordinate time

**Regime boundaries:**
- Diffuse field: Θ → 0 → time unmeasurable
- Dense, coherent field: Θ > 0 → emergent time

Time is born with field densification.
In pure diffuse (primordial Φ), there is no time —
only the potential for emergence.

---

### Prototypical Form (A_FORMALISER)

```
Θ(ρ,κ,m) = α · ρ^β · κ^γ
```

Constants α, β, γ to be extracted by the inter-AI constants program.
This power-law form is consistent with GPS data and biological longevity data.

---

### What Pch Adds Over Relativity

General relativity describes *how* time varies (metric formalism).
Pch says *why*: local field density and coherence govern the cadence
of any process — including atomic clocks.

Relativity is the operational limit of FT-11 in the dense condensed regime.
