## FT-0 — Primitive Field
**Status:** ACQUIRED
**Expression:** `Φ(x)`

---

### Statement

The field is the primitive entity.
Every structure, every phenomenon, and every measurement
derives from the state of the field.

Φ is not a potential in the classical physics sense.
It is the fundamental reality of which classical physics potentials
are regime-dependent projections.

---

### Parameters

**Φ** — single universal field
*x* — position (space, or space-time in the extended reading)

---

### What This Establishes

FT-0 is the ontological foundation of the Pch framework.
It does not describe a phenomenon — it establishes the substrate
from which all phenomena and all other formulas derive.

Without FT-0, the distinction Φc / Φd (FT-1) has no ground.
Without FT-0, ∇Φ (FT-3) is a mathematical operation on an undefined object.

FT-0 says: the field exists first. Everything else is a state of the field.

---

### Observable Grounding

Every experiment in the CMRPCh corpus is an observable of Φ.
No experiment requires a substrate other than Φ.

The discriminating power of FT-0 is indirect:
it is refuted not by a single experiment
but by any phenomenon that demonstrably requires
an ontological entity irreducible to a field state.

No such phenomenon has been identified.

---

### Falsification Criterion

FT-0 fails if:
any documented phenomenon requires a substrate
that cannot be described as a state of Φ.

---

### FT Activated By This Formula
All FTs — FT-0 is the substrate of the entire framework.
