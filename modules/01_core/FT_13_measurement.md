## FT-13 — Measurement as Local Condensation
**Status:** ACQUIRED
**Expression:** `Measure: Φd → Φc`

---

### Statement

An instrument converts diffuse field information into a local measurable structure.
Measurement does not exhaust field reality — it captures one partial aspect.
What is not measured is not absent.

---

### What This Resolves

In the standard framework (and in quantum mechanics specifically),
measurement is treated as a special event that "collapses" the wave function —
a process without clear mechanism.

In Pch: measurement is Φd → Φc condensation.
The "collapse" is not mysterious — it is the same transition
that produces matter from field at all scales.
The instrument is a local condensed system
that forces Φd information into a Φc readable structure.

---

### Observable Grounding

EXP-002 (Lewin dismantled capacitor) is the direct demonstration:

The Wimshurst machine generates Φd states in the two armatures.
Separated (condensator dismantled): each armature carries its state —
but there is no path toward the complement → no manifestation, no shock.
Touching each armature separately: neutral.

Reassembled: path restored → field condensation → spark.

The field was present throughout in Φd form.
The reassembly created the conditions for Φd → Φc transition.
That transition is the measurement / manifestation event.

---

### Falsification Criterion

FT-13 fails if:
measurement is demonstrated to not alter field state in any case —
i.e., Φd → Φc transition has no observable consequence.
