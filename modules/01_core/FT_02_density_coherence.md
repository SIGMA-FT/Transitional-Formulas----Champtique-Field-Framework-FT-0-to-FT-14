## FT-2 — Local Density and Coherence
**Status:** ACQUIRED
**Expression:** `ρ = ρ(Φ), κ = κ(Φ), κ ∈ [0, 1[`

---

### Statement

Two local parameters characterize the field state at any point:

**ρ** — effective field density at position x

**κ** — local coherence, degree of field organization

---

### Critical Constraint on κ

κ is bounded to **[0, 1[** — the upper bound κ = 1 is never reached locally.

Perfect absolute coherence (κ = 1) belongs to Φ global as an asymptotic limit.
Every local coherence is partial and evolving.

This is not a technical detail — it is structurally necessary.
A locally reachable κ = 1 would imply a locally closed, self-sufficient system,
which contradicts the continuum principle of Pch.

---

### κ Governs Memory Duration

High κ → τm → ∞ : information inscribes permanently
Low κ → τm finite : progressive dissolution of incoherent states

This is the direct connection to FT-8.

---

### Φd Absorption Hierarchy

Documented across experiments EXP-006, EXP-007, EXP-009:

```
water > living matter > air > vacuum
```

Materials with higher Φd absorption capacity show stronger
interface effects and more pronounced champtique signatures.

---

### Observable Grounding

- EXP-006, EXP-007: water/fiber interface — κ difference drives thermal direction
- OBS-006: surface state (polish) modifies κ → measurable effect on field coupling
- OBS-005: dense media → high ρ → high Θ → longer biological time (FT-11)

---

### Falsification Criterion

FT-2 fails if:
κ = 1 is reached and measured locally, or if
field behavior is demonstrated to be independent of κ in any documented case.
