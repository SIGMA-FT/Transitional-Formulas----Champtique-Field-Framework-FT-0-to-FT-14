## FT-9 — Matter as Stabilized Condensed Field
**Status:** A_DISC (Open)
**Expression:** `M ≡ C(Φc, ρ, κ, m)`

---

### Statement

Matter is a slow local memory of the field —
a stabilized configuration of Φc with a specific density ρ,
coherence κ, and history m.

C is a function not yet formalized.

---

### Parameters

**Φc** — local condensed field state
**ρ** — local field density
**κ** — local coherence
**m** — field memory (history)
**C** — stabilization function (open zone)

---

### Role in the Framework

FT-9 is the bridge between the field formalism and observable matter.

It says: what we call "a material" is not a primitive substance —
it is a field configuration with a specific (ρ, κ, m) signature.

Two materials with identical chemical composition but different thermal history
have different (κ, m) — and therefore different champtique behavior.
This is directly testable.

---

### Open Zone

**C is not yet formalized.**

This is the recognized frontier toward FT-15/16:
the unification of fundamental constants as regime-dependent projections
of the field state.

The open question: how do Φc, ρ, κ, m combine to produce
the observable material properties (conductivity, permeability,
mechanical resistance, biological compatibility)?

---

### Observable Candidates

**Annealed vs tempered copper (immediate experimental candidate):**

Same bar of copper — same mass, same chemical composition.
Annealing: slow cooling → κ relaxes toward local continuum → high κ, low internal m stress.
Tempering: rapid quench → κ frozen in high-energy state → lower κ, high internal m stress.

Pch prediction: braking of falling magnet differs between annealed and tempered bar
→ κ is modified by thermal history → C depends on m.

Standard framework prediction: same conductivity, same permeability →
same magnetic braking. No difference predicted.

This is a directly discriminating, accessible experiment.
Materials available. Protocol: identical to OBS-008.

**OBS-006 (surface state — Black Galaxy granite):**
Face polished vs rough on same block.
If behavior differs → κ is surface-dependent, not only bulk.
If identical → κ is volume-dominated.

**OBS-009 (geometry vs mass, iron powder vs cube):**
Same mass, different surface area.
Pch: force = f(interface surface) not f(mass).
Standard: force = f(mass).
Directly discriminating.

---

### Falsification Criterion

FT-9 fails if:
two materials with identical (Φc, ρ) but different (κ, m)
are demonstrated to have identical champtique behavior —
i.e., thermal and field history have no measurable effect.
