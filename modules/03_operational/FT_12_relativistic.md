## FT-12 — Relativistic Extension (Operational)
**Status:** A_EXP (Operational — regime approximation)
**Expression:** `dτ = Θ(ρ,κ,m) · √(1 − v²/c²) · dt`

---

### Statement

The local time cadence depends on both the field state (Θ)
and kinematic velocity (relativistic factor).

This formula extends FT-11 to include velocity effects,
for operational compatibility with the existing relativistic framework.

---

### Parameters

**Θ(ρ,κ,m)** — geometric-champtique factor (from FT-11)
**c** — limit velocity at the condensed/diffuse interface
**v** — velocity of the system
**dτ, dt** — local proper time, coordinate time

---

### Critical Note: c is Not a Universal Constant in Pch

In the standard framework, c is a universal constant —
identical everywhere, at all times, under all conditions.

**In Pch: c is the limit velocity at the Φc/Φd interface
under current terrestrial regime conditions.**

It is a regime-dependent parameter, not a primitive.
It may vary locally where the field state differs significantly
from terrestrial standard conditions.

FT-12 treats c as constant by operational approximation only.

---

### Why This Formula is OPERATIONAL, Not ACQUIRED

FT-12 cannot currently be demonstrated to be ontologically fundamental.

It is accepted because:
1. It produces correct results within its domain (GPS to 10⁻¹⁴)
2. It is compatible with the existing relativistic formalism
3. It reduces to FT-11 at v = 0 (correct limit)

It is not accepted because:
- c as constant is a regime approximation, not a Pch postulate
- Local variation of c has not yet been experimentally accessed

---

### Approximation Conditions

FT-12 is valid where:
- Local field state approximates terrestrial standard conditions
- Velocity is non-negligible relative to c
- Precision requirements are at the GPS correction level (10⁻¹⁴)

FT-12 may break down where:
- Local Φd/Φc ratio differs significantly from terrestrial standard
- Extreme field gradients modify the effective c locally

---

### Observable Candidate for Future Discrimination

**PH-059 (guided laser propagation over calm water):**

If the propagation velocity of the laser signal varies measurably
with the surface coherence κ (calm vs agitated water),
this constitutes indirect evidence of local c variation with κ.

Technically difficult with current instrumentation.
Flagged as a future discriminating measurement.

---

### Falsification Criterion

FT-12 fails if:
c is demonstrated to vary locally in conditions where
the Pch prediction for field state is well-defined —
and that variation exceeds the approximation tolerance.

*(Note: this would strengthen Pch, not refute it —
it would confirm that c is regime-dependent as postulated.
The operational formula would then require a corrected form.)*
