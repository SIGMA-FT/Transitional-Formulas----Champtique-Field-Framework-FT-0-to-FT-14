# CMRPCh-Formulas — Abstract
## Transitional Formulas — Champtique Field Framework FT-0 to FT-14

---

This deposit documents the minimal formal structure of the single-field
framework developed across CMRPCh Deposits 1–4.

The structural reading rests on three postulates — not formulas —
which have priority over all FTs in case of apparent conflict:

```
P1 — The field is the fundamental reality
     Matter and energy are states of a single field Φ

P2 — Field plasticity
     Φ reconfigures between states under observable conditions
     This is a mechanism, not a metaphor

P3 — No opposing charges
     High-density and low-density are two states of a single continuum
     The 1785 bifurcation (Deposit 2) selected the two-fluid model
     on non-empirical grounds — the single-field reading is restored here
```

**Fifteen transitional formulas (FT-0 to FT-14)** are organized
into four methodological categories:

**ACQUIRED (11 formulas)** — confirmed observational grounding,
validated across Deposits 1–4 and by independent AI structural analysis:

```
FT-0  : Φ(x)                          — primitive field
FT-1  : Φ = Φc + Φd                   — foundational decomposition
FT-2  : κ ∈ [0,1[                     — local coherence variable
FT-3  : O ∝ ∇Φ                        — observable as field gradient
FT-5  : J = −∂tΦ                      — flux without material transport
FT-6  : ∂tρ + ∇·J = S − R            — redistribution, not dissipation
FT-8  : τm = f(κ)                     — field memory depends on coherence
FT-10 : Φd precedes Φc               — structural non-locality
FT-11 : dτ = Θ(ρ,κ,m)·dt            — local variable time cadence
FT-13 : Measure: Φd → Φc             — measurement as local condensation
FT-14 : ΔΦ = Φ₁ − Φ₂                — champtique differential as driver
```

**OPEN (3 formulas)** — structurally necessary, not yet fully formalized:
FT-4 (response function), FT-7 (field dynamics), FT-9 (matter state).
Open zones explicitly acknowledged.

**OPERATIONAL (1 formula)** — regime approximation, not ontological law:
FT-12 (relativistic extension — c treated as regime limit, not universal constant).

**FORTHCOMING** — prototypical forms for constants extraction program.
An inter-AI research program for numerical extraction of Pch constants
(Θ, τm functional forms) is announced as a structured research subject.
